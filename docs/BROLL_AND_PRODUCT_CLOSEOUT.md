# B-Roll And Product Closeout

## B-Roll Is Not Filler

B-roll should give the edit air, rhythm, and world detail. It should not be leftover subject footage.

Good b-roll jobs:

- product texture
- material macro
- empty architecture with weather
- moving light over surfaces
- water, glass, fabric, chrome, stone, leather
- machinery or controls as texture
- practical lights
- breath between subject-driven impacts

## B-Roll Generation

- Generate short inserts when the edit only needs 1-2 seconds.
- Four-second nodes are often more useful than multiple 10-second b-roll clips.
- Prompt b-roll with a specific visual job.
- Do not call crop-derived proxy b-roll final.

## Product Closeout

If the campaign is an ad, the final card must make that unmistakable.

Rules:

- Generate a clean product frame without baked-in text.
- Make the product the hero, not incidental styling.
- Use the same material/light language as the film.
- Add brand/product/tagline/CTA typography deterministically.
- Keep enough negative space for type.
- Avoid generated fake labels, logos, or watermarks.

## Closeout Prompt Pattern

`Clean product-first end-card plate. [Product] on [surface] with [lighting], [campaign color/mood], large negative space for deterministic typography, no text, no logo, no watermark.`
