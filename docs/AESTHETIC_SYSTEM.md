# Aesthetic System

Premium AI campaign work is usually not expensive because it has more detail. It feels expensive because it has hierarchy, restraint, control, and a point of view.

## Core Rules

- Use one dominant visual idea per shot.
- Give every frame a job.
- Make the product or subject hierarchy obvious.
- Let materials carry taste: glass, wool, chrome, leather, rain, stone, skin, paper, smoke, water, metal.
- Use camera grammar deliberately: locked tableau, macro proof, low-angle power, direct portrait, lateral tracking, overhead geometry.
- Do not let motion exist only because the model can move.
- Keep text deterministic.

## Expensive Signals

- specific locations instead of generic backdrops
- restrained color systems
- clean silhouettes
- decisive posture and blocking
- tactile surfaces
- controlled practical light
- negative space
- a memorable final hold

## Cheap Signals

- vague “luxury cinematic” prompting
- overstuffed scenes
- random slow motion
- unmotivated camera orbit
- repeated shot shapes
- fake signage or AI text
- product drift
- broken hands, reflections, or physical contact
- no clear final memory image

## Generation vs Editing

Generate stillframes, styleframes, product plates, environment plates, and motion clips. Edit typography, logos, captions, legal lines, end cards, timing, transitions, and final grade deterministically.
