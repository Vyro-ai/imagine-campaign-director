# Music-Led Editing

Music is not decoration. For campaign work, it is the edit spine.

## Before Motion

Create the Music Studio prompt before final motion prompting. Decide:

- duration
- tempo
- mood
- genre
- instrumentation
- vocal/no vocal
- intended edit hits
- final hold moment

## Beat Map

For any music-led spot, define rough hit points:

- opening impact
- first cut
- product reveal
- detail accent
- final hold

Use broad timing if exact beats are not known yet.

## Editing Rules

- Let hard cuts land on major phrases.
- Use smaller reframes, flashes, blooms, or shakes on secondary accents.
- Do not shake or flash on every beat.
- Vary transitions.
- Keep campaign text minimal unless the brief needs information.
- Reserve final typography for product, brand, offer, or CTA.

## Music Studio Prompt Rule

The Music Studio prompt should be written from the campaign grammar, not from generic genre words alone.

Weak: `cinematic luxury music`

Stronger: `15-second instrumental bed for a black orchid fragrance film, slow 72 BPM pulse, soft sub bass, glass synth texture, distant low strings, one warm shimmer on the final bottle hold, no vocals.`
