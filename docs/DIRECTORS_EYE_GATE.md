# Director's Eye Gate

Use this after still anchors are generated and before campaign motion launch. This gate is about taste in the selected frames, not just prompt variety.

## Core Rule

Approved anchors must look like a director and fashion/product editor chose them. A contact sheet of technically consistent but interchangeable poses is a failure.

Do not approve a still set when the subject is essentially:

- standing front-facing or three-quarter in every frame
- wearing the same expression in every frame
- centered at the same height in every frame
- using the same shoulder angle, hand position, and weight distribution
- in the same corridor, room, tabletop, or background with only crop changes
- lit by the same rim light, reflection, or packshot setup in every frame

## Contact Sheet Review

Before motion launch, inspect approved anchors as a contact sheet with prompts hidden:

| Dimension | Pass Requirement | Fail Signal |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Blocking | body/object position changes with story purpose | same stance or packshot repeated |
| Composition | frame geometry changes deliberately | same centered crop |
| Performance | gaze, posture, gesture, and attitude evolve | same blank expression |
| Lens/Distance | wide, medium, close, macro, and final hold serve different jobs | crop variations only |
| Spatial Depth | foreground, midground, and background are used intentionally | flat backdrop |
| Editorial Contrast | adjacent shots create a cut reason | adjacent shots feel swappable |
| Physical Event | several frames show something happening | all frames are static |
| Final Image | last frame feels earned and memorable | just another nice frame |

If two adjacent anchors could be swapped without changing the film, redesign one.

## Blocking Matrix

For every subject shot, document:

| Shot | Body/object angle | Weight/stance | Hands/contact | Gaze/facing | Movement path | Camera mark | Spatial relationship |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Reject when more than two subject shots share the same body/object angle, gaze/facing, and hand/contact state.

## Fashion Authority Lock

For fashion, beauty, and lifestyle work, define:

- silhouette
- material behavior
- styling thesis
- posture language
- hair/makeup restraint
- forbidden pose family

Every subject shot must serve at least one of those. Showing the model is not enough.

## Six-Up Squint Test

View the strongest six anchors small, with prompts hidden. If the campaign can be summarized as `beautiful model/product in moody luxury lighting`, status is `needs revision`.

## Director's Veto Pass

Before motion launch, answer:

`What makes this unmistakably directed rather than merely varied?`

Reject if the answer is only shot-type variety, prettier lighting, or generic mood.

## Motion Launch Blockers

Block motion launch when:

- the contact sheet looks like one lookbook pose or packshot repeated
- anchors do not express the creative spine
- there is no visible status change from beginning to final hold
- the model/product has no performance or reveal progression
- motion prompts ask Seedance to invent the directing missing from the stills
