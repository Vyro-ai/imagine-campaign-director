# Reference Analysis Method

References should sharpen judgment, not become templates to copy.

## Observe

Study:

- opening hook
- shot sizes
- camera angle and movement
- pacing
- transition logic
- wardrobe and posture
- art direction
- surfaces and materials
- lighting and color
- product presentation
- final frame

## Convert

Convert references into general principles:

- `reduction`: fewer elements, stronger hierarchy
- `ritual scale`: choreography, landscape, and symbolic props
- `product mythology`: product framed through a world, object, or gesture
- `industrial precision`: technical surfaces and controlled movement
- `maximal theatre`: clutter controlled by performance and hierarchy

## Do Not Copy

Do not redistribute media, include copyrighted frames, transcribe protected material, or recreate a reference one-to-one. Do not imply endorsement by referenced brands or creators.

## Document Ethically

Write down the reusable lesson, not the protected expression. Replace real brands and scenes with fictional examples when publishing prompt packs or templates.
