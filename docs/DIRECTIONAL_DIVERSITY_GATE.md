# Directional Diversity Gate

Use this before still generation and again before motion launch. It prevents a mechanically valid workflow from becoming a sequence of interchangeable images.

## Core Test

The shot ladder must read like a directed film, not prompt variations around the same person, object, hallway, lens, and pose.

For a 20-30 second fashion, beauty, lifestyle, product, or luxury campaign, the plan must contain clear contrast across:

- shot size: wide/world, medium action, portrait, macro/detail, final hero
- camera grammar: track, approach, reveal, orbit/arc, tilt, locked hold, motivated cutaway
- subject action: enter, cross, turn, reveal, touch/use product, pause, look, exit, hold
- environment event: door/elevator, water/rain, light sweep, reflection wipe, vehicle, crowd edge, fabric/wind, surface interaction
- visual job: hook, world, product/wardrobe truth, tactile detail, escalation, breath, attitude, closeout

## Repetition Rejects

Reject or redesign before spending credits when:

- more than two adjacent shots use the same shot size, subject posture, location, and camera move
- more than 40% of the planned runtime is static or near-static portrait coverage
- the contact sheet can be summarized as `same model in same outfit in same corridor`
- multiple anchors differ only by crop while serving the same visual job
- the only planned action is a slow push-in or the model standing still
- the environmental breath is just an empty version of the same location without a new light, reflection, weather, or spatial event

## Campaign Spark Requirements

For each 30-second campaign, include at least five distinct high-value events or production design beats before motion launch. Examples:

- elevator doors or glass doors opening into the world
- heel/foot crossing a sharp pool of practical light
- coat catching wind, rain, or turn momentum
- chrome reflection wipe that motivates a cut
- macro material detail with a visible physical event
- model entering from silhouette into face light
- camera passes behind foreground glass or architecture
- light sweep travels across wet floor and garment edge
- final hero hold with a clear posture change or lighting reveal

If a planned event is too risky for AI motion, simplify it. Do not delete all events and leave only static portraits.

## Treatment Table Addition

Every director's treatment must include a diversity table:

| Shot | Shot size | Camera grammar | Subject action | Environment event | Visual job |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

No two adjacent rows may be identical across more than three columns. If they are, merge or redesign one shot before building the workflow.

## Contact Sheet Review

Before motion launch, make or inspect a contact sheet of approved anchors. Status must be `needs revision` if the anchors do not show meaningful variation in scale, angle, environment, action, and editorial purpose.
