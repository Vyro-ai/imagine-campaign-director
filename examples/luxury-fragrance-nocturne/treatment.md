# Director's Treatment

Campaign thesis: The fragrance is evidence; the sound of handling arrives before the romance of seeing it.

Selected creative spine: A smoked glass bottle is partially hidden on a clinical metal tray until object percussion and silence force the final reveal.

Rejected premises: Blue-hour wet pavement was rejected as expected fragrance language. Human-trace romance was rejected because it made the product secondary. Product monument was rejected because it lacked sequence.

Direction DNA: Jonathan Glazer clinical distance translated into cool observation, precise blocking, quiet threat, and abrupt sonic punctuation.

Controlled flaw: The tray edge occludes the bottle until the final hit.

Aesthetic refusals: no wet pavement, no orchid petals, no smoke swirl, no centered bottle before the final hit.

Music spine: glass stopper click, metal tray ring, sparse sub, dry snare on scale needle, silence before final shimmer.

Seedance plan: two Seedance 2 nodes, reference-image set mode, beat ladder notes in workflow, final product plate tied to the audio dropout.
