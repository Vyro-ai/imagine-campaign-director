# Director's Treatment

Campaign thesis: The Paris fragrance test case is a production-readiness example, so the creative direction must still name a spine rather than act as a generic nocturne.

Selected creative spine: A black orchid fragrance moves through old-stone night as a sequence of controlled evidence: bottle, street, hand, surface, final lockup.

Rejected premises: A pure bottle loop was rejected for insufficient editorial progression. A crowded Paris romance was rejected for identity and crowd risk. A smoke-heavy perfume reveal was rejected for artifact risk.

Direction DNA: restrained nocturnal editorial grammar translated into wet stone, amber practicals, narrow camera moves, and product-first hierarchy without copying a specific campaign.

Controlled flaw: practical-lamp falloff and wet-stone obstruction keep the frame from feeling over-polished.

Aesthetic refusals: no fake storefront text, no crowd spectacle, no smoke-heavy reveal, no centered product before the closeout.

Music spine: slow atmospheric pulse, glass synth texture, low strings, one shimmer for the final product lock.

Seedance plan: image-grounded Seedance 2 nodes with approved still anchors, simplified hand movement, local export verification, and deterministic final type.
